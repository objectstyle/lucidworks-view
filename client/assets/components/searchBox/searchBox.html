Search Tips

Union

  • cats dogs
  • cats OR dogs

Intersect

Most of the time, you will want to remember to explicitly AND terms together:

  • cats AND dogs
  • +cats +dogs

Nesting

(+cats +dogs) (+"peanut butter" +jelly)

Subtraction

Minus (-) excludes a term from results, and automatically ANDs it with the rest of the query.

  • cats -dogs
  • cats AND NOT dogs

Union

cats dogs
cats OR dogs

Phrases

"grumpy cat"

Wildcards

Question mark (?) matches a single, arbitrary character.

Asterisk (*) matches any word or phrase.

  • cats
  • c?ts
  • +khtml +like +Gecko
  • +khtml +like +Geck?
  • "khtml like Geck\?"
  • +"khtml, like" +Ge*
  • "khtml, like \*"
  • error\:
Notes:
  1. Wildcards and other special characters (e.g., +, -, &, |, !, (, ), {, }, [, ], ^, ", ~, *, ?, :, and \) need to be escaped (e.g., \?, \?) when used inside phrases/strings, or searched for as a literal.
  2. An asterisk cannot be used as the first character of a term (e.g., *oogle is bad syntax).

Fuzzy searches

Lucene can search for similar terms: integer~ will match on integer, integers, and intejer.

Specify a threshold

An optional fuzziness threshold can be specified, from 0.0 (very loose) to 1.0 (very strict).

  • integer~
  • integer~0.5
  • integer~0.4
  • integer~0.6
